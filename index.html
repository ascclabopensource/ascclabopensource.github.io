ASCC HomeSoundNet

1. Introduction

This website contains the ASCC HomeSoundNet Dataset, which is a dataset of home event sounds. At this point, this dataset consists of 36 categories of sound events. There are a total of 31,423 audio clips with a total time of 138,522 seconds. The audios clips are collected from our ASCC Smart Home Testbed, and online existing datasets, including Google Audioset [1], CHIME-HOME [2], SINS [3] and Eating Sound Collection [4]. We also used web crawler technique to search and download related audios uploaded by users from all over the world from Freesound [5]. To collect our own audio data, a laptop was used to record the audios at different distances and in different rooms.

The original audio clips from different sources have different sampling rates, durations and channels. These data are processed and unified into two sampling frequencies: 32kHz and 8kHz with each audio piece having less than 5 seconds. The sampling rate of 8kHz results in less data, which fits for systems that have less computational resources, such as wearable computing devices. All the audio clips are manually verified and those with no sound event or unnoticeable sound event are removed.
